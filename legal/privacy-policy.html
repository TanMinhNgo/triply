Privacy Policy

Last updated: [EFFECTIVE DATE] · This policy explains what data Triply collects and how we use it.

This Privacy Policy describes how [LEGAL ENTITY] ("Triply," "we," "us") collects, uses, stores, and shares personal data when you use the Triply mobile app (the "Service"). It covers the app itself; our marketing website collects no personal data and sets no cookies or trackers. By using the Service, you agree to the practices described here.

1. Data we collect

Account and identity data

You sign in with Google or Apple through our authentication provider, Clerk. When you do, we receive and store a user identifier, your email address, your name, and your profile image URL as provided by that sign-in provider. We do not receive or store a password — sign-in is handled entirely by Google, Apple, and Clerk.

Trip and content data

When you plan a trip, we collect the inputs you provide: destination, travel dates, number of days and travelers, budget level, interests, and pace. We store the AI-generated itinerary, budget estimate, and cover image associated with each trip. If you choose to set a custom cover image, we access the photo you select from your device's photo library and upload it to our image host. We also store the messages you send to, and receive from, the AI travel assistant and the trip-refine chat.

Usage data

We keep a simple per-day counter of how many trips you generate, to enforce an anti-abuse limit.

Diagnostic, telemetry, and session-replay data

To monitor errors and performance, the app uses Sentry, which automatically collects diagnostic data about your use of the app. As currently configured, this includes:

  • technical event and performance data, and your IP address and related request context;
  • structured logs of certain in-app actions — for example, that a trip generation started (including the destination you entered) and that a sign-in occurred (including which provider you used);
  • for AI assistant conversations, monitoring data that includes the content of the messages you send to the assistant and the assistant's replies;
  • session replays for a sample of app sessions and for sessions where an error occurs. These replays can capture on-screen text and images, because screen masking is not enabled.

Diagnostic data is processed by Sentry on our behalf and is currently sent to Sentry's European Union region.

Data we do not collect

The app does not collect your precise location or GPS coordinates, does not use advertising or marketing analytics or ad-tracking SDKs, does not build an advertising profile of you, does not send push notifications, and does not process any payment or financial information (the Service is free and contains no payment functionality). Map locations shown in the app are AI-estimated points of interest, not your location.

2. How we use your data

  • to authenticate you and provide access to your account;
  • to generate, store, display, and let you refine your trips and itineraries;
  • to operate the AI travel assistant;
  • to enforce usage limits and protect the Service against abuse;
  • to monitor, debug, secure, and improve the Service (error tracking, performance, and session diagnostics);
  • to communicate with you about support requests you send us.

We do not sell your personal data, and we do not use it for third-party advertising.

3. AI processing of your inputs

To generate itineraries and assistant replies, we send your trip inputs and your chat messages to our AI provider, OpenAI, which processes them to produce responses. Please avoid sending sensitive personal information in chat messages or trip inputs, as this content is transmitted to OpenAI and stored by us as part of your trips and transcripts. OpenAI's handling of this data is governed by its own terms and privacy commitments.

4. Third-party services that process your data

We rely on the following service providers ("processors" / "sub-processors") to run the Service. Each receives only the data needed for its function and processes it under its own terms and privacy policy:

  • Clerk — authentication and account identity (identifier, email, name, avatar);
  • Google / Apple — sign-in providers that authenticate you and share basic profile data;
  • OpenAI — receives your trip inputs and chat messages to generate itineraries and assistant responses;
  • Neon — hosts the database where your account, trips, and chats are stored;
  • ImageKit — hosts and delivers trip cover images, including any image you upload;
  • Unsplash — receives your destination as a search query to supply stock cover photos;
  • Inngest — runs background jobs that generate trips and sync account changes;
  • Sentry — receives the diagnostic, telemetry, and session-replay data described in Section 1;
  • Apple Maps (via the device) — renders map locations of suggested places;
  • Expo — the underlying app runtime and platform.

5. How your data is stored and secured

Your account and trip data are stored in our hosted Postgres database (Neon). Uploaded and stock cover images are stored with our image host (ImageKit). We apply reasonable technical measures, including: encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS) for data in transit; short-lived signed session tokens that are verified on every request and stored in your device's secure storage (the device keychain); signature verification of incoming account webhooks; and access scoping so that queries return only the data belonging to the authenticated account. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Data retention

We keep your account and trip data for as long as your account exists, and until you delete it or your account. When you delete your account, we delete your account record and associated trips, itineraries, and chats from our database.   • Delete individual trips (which also removes their chat messages).
  • Clear your AI assistant chat history.
  • Sign out at any time.
